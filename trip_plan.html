Start · Day 1
Denver, Colorado
Gear up, fill the tank, grab breakfast on Colfax. Hit I-70 West early — it's a 6-hour drive to Moab and you'll want daylight when you arrive in canyon country.
Departure
🚗 6 hrs · 360 mi — I-70 W + US-191 S to Moab
★★
Days 2–3 · 1 night in Moab
Arches National Park
Full day — Delicate Arch hike (start before 7am to beat the heat and crowds), Windows Section, Balanced Rock. Over 2,000 natural sandstone arches. Timed entry reservations required in summer — book months in advance at recreation.gov.
Days 2–3 · 1 night ⭐ 4.8
🚗 30 min · 20 mi — Moab to Canyonlands Island in the Sky
★
Day 3 · Half Day
Canyonlands National Park
Half-day drive-by — Mesa Arch at sunrise perfectly frames the canyon below, Grand View Point overlook. Then continue southwest toward Bryce. Worth the short detour from Moab.
Day 3 · half day ⭐ 4.8
🚗 3.5 hrs · 200 mi — UT-12 Scenic Byway to Bryce Canyon
★★
Days 4–5 · 1–2 nights
Bryce Canyon National Park
1.5 days — sunrise at Sunrise Point, Navajo Loop down into the hoodoos, Rim Trail to Inspiration Point. One of the most photogenic parks on earth and the best park-per-hour ratio on the entire route. Don't skip it.
Days 4–5 · 1–2 nights ⭐ 4.9
🚗 1.5 hrs · 83 mi — US-89 S to Zion
★★
Days 6–7 · 2 nights
Zion National Park
2 days — Angels Landing (permit lottery required at recreation.gov), Canyon Overlook Trail, Riverside Walk into The Narrows. One of the most visited parks in the US — book lodging months in advance. The summer shuttle makes the main canyon car-free.
Days 6–7 · 2 nights ⭐ 4.8
🚗 4.5 hrs · 300 mi — I-15 N to Salt Lake City, then I-84 W toward Boise
⛽
Day 8 · Transit
Salt Lake City → Boise
Long haul north. Overnight in Twin Falls or Boise to break up the drive. Twin Falls has Shoshone Falls nearby if you want a quick detour.
Transit night
🚗 3 hrs from Boise · 220 mi — US-20 W + OR-62 to Crater Lake
★★
Days 9–10 · 1 night
Crater Lake National Park
Full Rim Drive (33 miles), Watchman Peak hike, Cleetwood Cove trail to the water's edge. The deepest lake in the US, filled entirely by rain and snow — the blue is surreal in person. Worth every mile of Oregon detour.
Days 9–10 · 1 night ⭐ 4.9
🚗 4.5 hrs · 290 mi — OR-138 W + I-5 N + US-12 E to Rainier
★
Days 11–12 · 1 night
Mount Rainier National Park
Skyline Trail loop from Paradise, Myrtle Falls, Reflection Lakes. Peak wildflowers bloom July–August — timing this trip right means an unforgettable finish. Then 2.5 hrs into Seattle.
Days 11–12 · 1 night ⭐ 4.9
🚗 2.5 hrs · 90 mi — WA-7 N + I-5 N to Seattle
■
Day 12–13 · Finish
Seattle, Washington
Pike Place Market, fresh salmon, rest, and legendary stories with your cousins. Well earned.
Final destination

⚠️ Book these early for Summer 2027

  • Arches NP — Timed entry reservations open months in advance and sell out fast (recreation.gov).
  • Zion — Angels Landing — Permit lottery required. Apply early at recreation.gov.
  • Zion lodging — Book 6+ months out. Fills completely in summer.
  • Crater Lake lodging — Limited options, books quickly for July–August.

Start · Day 1
Denver, Colorado
Early departure recommended — it's a full 7-hour drive to Jackson Hole. Grab coffee and get moving. The Tetons will make it worth it.
Departure
🚗 7 hrs · 440 mi — I-25 N + US-287 N to Jackson, WY
★★
Days 2–3 · 2 nights in Jackson
Grand Teton National Park
1.5–2 days — Jenny Lake loop, Oxbow Bend for moose and elk at dawn, Snake River Overlook. Arguably the most dramatic mountain skyline in the continental US. Jackson Hole is an excellent base for food and lodging.
Days 2–3 · 2 nights ⭐ 4.9
🚗 1 hr · 60 mi — US-89/191 N straight into Yellowstone's south entrance
★★
Days 4–5 · 2 nights inside the park
Yellowstone National Park
2 full days — Old Faithful and the Upper Geyser Basin, Grand Prismatic Spring (hike the Fairy Falls trail for the aerial view), Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone, Lamar Valley wildlife drive at dawn and dusk for bison, wolves, and bears. Book lodging 13 months in advance.
Days 4–5 · 2 nights ⭐ 4.8
🚗 4 hrs · 245 mi — US-89 N + US-93 N to Kalispell, MT
⛽
Day 6 · Transit
Kalispell, Montana
Gateway town to Glacier. Good restaurants, easy overnight. Stock up on snacks and bear spray before hitting the park tomorrow.
Transit night
🚗 35 min · 30 mi — US-2 E to Glacier's West Glacier entrance
★★
Days 7–8 · 2 nights
Glacier National Park
2 days — Going-to-the-Sun Road (50-mile alpine drive, vehicle permit required — book February 2027, sells out in hours), Grinnell Glacier hike, Hidden Lake Overlook. Raw, wild, and utterly spectacular.
Days 7–8 · 2 nights ⭐ 4.8
🚗 5 hrs · 272 mi — I-90 W to Spokane
⛽
Day 12 · Overnight
Spokane, Washington
Big drive day to Spokane before going to Mount Rainier the next day.
Transit night
🚗 5 hrs · 300 mi — I-90 E to Rainier's entrance
★
Day 11 · Half Day
Mount Rainier National Park
Half to full day — Paradise Visitor Center, Skyline Trail loop, Myrtle Falls. Peak wildflowers bloom July–August. Then 2 hrs into Seattle for the night before heading to Olympic.
Day 11 · half day ⭐ 4.9

⛽
Day 12 · Overnight
Seattle, Washington
Overnight in the city before the Olympic Peninsula finale. Pike Place Market, fresh Dungeness crab, and a well-earned rest before the final leg.
Transit night
🚗 3 hrs · 105 mi — US-101 W to Olympic's Hoh entrance
■
Days 13–14 · Finish
Olympic National Park
1.5 days — Hoh Rain Forest Hall of Mosses, Rialto Beach sea stacks and tide pools, Hurricane Ridge for mountain views. Three completely different ecosystems in one park — rainforest, alpine, and rugged Pacific coastline. The perfect wild ending.
Days 13–14 · Final destination ⭐ 4.8

⚠️ Book these early for Summer 2027

  • Glacier — Going-to-the-Sun Road vehicle reservations open February 2027. Sell out within hours — set a calendar reminder now.
  • Yellowstone lodging — Opens 13 months in advance. Book as early as possible; in-park lodges fill completely for summer.
  • Grand Teton lodging — Book 6+ months out. Jackson Hole fills fast with summer visitors.
